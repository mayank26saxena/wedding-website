Jaipur is easily accessible - by air, by train, or by road. Or if you’re really adventurous, even by a camel.

By Air

Jaipur International Airport is located ~1 hr away from Samode Bagh and has access to all major flights to different cities.

By Train

Jaipur Railway Station offers direct trains to all major cities of the country. If wondering how to reach Jaipur from Delhi by train, trains such as Shatabdi, Rajdhani, and Duronto are good options.

By Road

Jaipur is connected to all major cities via roads. You can take a bus or hire a cab from nearby cities. If you’re coming from Delhi, it will take you ~5 hours (or more if traffic). The road is scenic and the views are gorgeous.

Where To Shop?

Jaipur is a shopper’s paradise, offering everything from a gorgeous variety of textiles to fine jewelry.

  • If you’re up for an adventure to shop in the local markets and can brave the crowds, head to Johari Bazaar, Bapu Bazaar, Nehru Bazaar.
  • Textiles: Apart from local shops (that we’d highly recommend trying first), you can head to stores like Anokhi, Cottons, Kilol, and Idli (hand-block textiles with a modern twist).
  • Jewelry: Johari Bazaar has many small stores that offer gorgeous jewelry made of gold, silver, and precious gemstones. Remember to always bargain. Or if you prefer, you can visit more established stores like Amrapali Jewels.

This guide can be helpful for shopping: Guide to Shopping in Jaipur or hit us up for recommendations!
